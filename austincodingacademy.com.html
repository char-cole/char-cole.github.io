austincodingacademy.com - static site with AMP JavaScript

Link to Austin Coding Academy

austincodingacademy.com is the marketing site and student portal for Austin Coding Academy, a local coding bootcamp. It is a static site written with Jekyll, with content populated by forestry.io, and utilizing Google's AMP JavaScript library.

Charles built the first version of the currently live site in December 2018, and continued to develop and maintain the site as the school grew and changed until May, 2019.

Developing this site was an exciting challenge for Charles because he was completely new to Google's webmaster tools and AMP (formerly Accelerated Mobile Pages, a streamlined and SEO-focused JavaScript library). Working within the framework of Google's guidelines for SEO, as well as overall minimizing the size and scripting of the site, meant that the most obvious approaches to goals were not always the most appropriate.

I wish I could tell you more, but nothing else has been written yet. Soon, this page will be an illustrated journal of the work Charles has done on this project.
