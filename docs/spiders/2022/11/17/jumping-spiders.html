Jumping Spiders: An Introduction

Jumping spiders are some of nature's most fascinating and brilliant creatures. They are incredibly charismatic and are widely considered some of the cutest "bugs" on Earth. They are so curious, fascinating to watch, and safe to handle that they make quite good pets - although they require more specialized care than most mammals.

All 6,000+ jumping spider species are members of the family Salticidae, the largest family of spiders. Unlike most spiders, which typically have quite poor eyesight, jumping spiders actually have excellent vision for their size - due in part to their exceptionally large anterior median eyes (front and center in their face), one of their most easily recognizable features.

Jumping spiders are also known for their elaborate courtship displays and, in some cases, their highly advanced hunting strategies. I have unfortunately not yet captured any of these famous behaviors, but I am still delighted any time I get to observe one. And, fortunately, they are often found in and around my home.
