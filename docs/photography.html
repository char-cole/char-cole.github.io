This is my photography blog, where I share and describe some of my favorite photos. Almost all of my photography is "macro", meaning high-magnification photos of very small things.

The most common subject of my blog is spiders. Some posts will be be about insects, gastropods, or microbes. Actually, hymenoptera (wasps, ants, and bees) and coleoptera (beetles) are some of my favorite creatures. But spiders make a better hobby for several reasons: they are common in wide variety, they are badly misunderstood by the public, and identification resources are much more widely available.

Equipment

Most of my photos were taken on a Pixel 3 or Pixel 5. Some of those were additionally taken through a 10x magnification hand lens - these are typically distinguishable by a very shallow focus (where most of the subject will be out of focus and the background will have pronounced bokeh).

I recently upgraded with a Sony a7 II digital camera & FE 90mm F2.8 Macro G OSS lens. While the power of the a7 is immediately apparent to me and I've taken many photographs impossible for a smartphone, the learning curve is significant. No quality guarantees during the learning period.
