Subjects

Class Arachnida (arachnids)

My most common subject is spiders (order Araneae), although this section also contains a handful of subjects from other orders of class Arachnida. I convinced myself to overcome a terrible case of arachnophobia when I was younger so that I could travel and spend more time in nature. After becoming comfortable around spiders, over time I came to feel tremendous respect and fascination for them. Because they are badly misunderstood by the public, and because there are many resources for spider identification, I now make spidering one of my main hobbies. The primary purpose of this catalogue is to be a repository for my spider identifications.

Class Insecta (insects)

Before I developed such a deep interest in spiders, I was photographing insects. With such tremendous variety in their forms, insects make excellent subjects. Indeed some of my favorite animals are hymenoptera (ants, bees, and wasps) and coleoptera (beetles). This section is smaller and receives fewer updates than arachnida, but contains some of my favorite images. (And, for arachnophobic readers, this section may be more enjoyable.)

misc. microbes

The diversity of microbial life one can see in ordinary samples is very high, spread across all kingdoms and domains of life. This section contains algae, plants, bacteria, animals, fungus, and more. (Technically, so do the others, but they focus on the animals.) In addition, identification of these organisms is much more difficult, so I'm rarely able to be very specific.

Equipment

Most of my photos were taken on a Pixel 3 or Pixel 5. Some of those were additionally taken through a 40x hand lens - these are typically distinguishable by a very shallow focus (where most of the subject will be out of focus and the background will have pronounced bokeh).

I recently upgraded with a Sony a7 II digital camera & FE 90mm F2.8 Macro G OSS lens. These have allowed me to capture detail and shots impossible for a smartphone, but the learning curve is significant.

My microscope has a 10x eyepiece lens and four objective lenses at 4x, 10x, 40x, and 100x. The total magnification of the subject is thus from 40x to 1000x. However, all photos taken through my microscope are taken with my Pixel 5 and further magnified by its lens.

Terminology

Macro photography is the practice of photographing very small organisms or objects with very high magnification so the subject appears greater than life size. Other terms for this include microphotography (but this can also mean the practice of shrinking physical photographs to tiny sizes), photomacrography, or macrography.

Microscopy is the practice of using a microscope. Although taking photos through a microscope is a form of macro photography, it also has a specific name: photomicrography.
