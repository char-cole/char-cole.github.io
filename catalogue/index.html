This is my photography catalogue, where I record encounters with many of my favorite creatures. Almost all of my photography is so-called "macrophotography", meaning high-magnification photos of very small things, and my subjects are typically animals of the phylum arthropoda.

I also have a section of photographs taken through a microscope, called _photomicrography_. Though confusing, the name is necessary to distinguish this technique from microphotography - an alternate, more accurate name for macrophotography.

Subjects

Class Arachnida (arachnids)

My most common subject is spiders (order Araneae), although this section also contains a handful of subjects from other orders of class Arachnida. I convinced myself to overcome a terrible case of arachnophobia when I was younger so that I could travel and spend more time in nature. After becoming comfortable around spiders, over time I came to feel tremendous respect and fascination for them. Because they are badly misunderstood by the public, and because there are many resources for spider identification, I now make spidering one of my main hobbies. The primary purpose of this catalogue is to be a repository for my spider identifications.

Class Insecta (insects)

Before I developed such a deep interest in spiders, I was photographing insects. With such tremendous variety in their forms, insects make excellent subjects. Indeed some of my favorite animals are hymenoptera (ants, bees, and wasps) and coleoptera (beetles). This section is smaller and receives fewer updates than arachnida, but contains some of my favorite images. (And, for arachnophobic readers, this section may be more enjoyable.)

misc. microbes

Photomicrography (taking pictures through a microscope) has its own section, because the variety of microbial life one can see in ordinary samples is very high, spread across all kingdoms and domains of life. This catalogue contains algae, plants, bacteria, animals, fungus, and more.

Because I spend less time with microbes than macroorganisms, it would be very difficult to fill out classes, let alone families. In addition, I don't have the education or specialized tools & techniques to confidently identify many microbes, even to a genus level in many cases. So this catalogue is all contained in a single page, rather than separated as arachnids and insects are.

Equipment

Most of my photos were taken on a Pixel 3 or Pixel 5. Some of those were additionally taken through a 10x magnification hand lens - these are typically distinguishable by a very shallow focus (where most of the subject will be out of focus and the background will have pronounced bokeh).

I recently upgraded with a Sony a7 II digital camera & FE 90mm F2.8 Macro G OSS lens. While the power of the a7 is immediately apparent to me and I've taken many photographs impossible for a smartphone, the learning curve is significant. No quality guarantees during the learning period.
