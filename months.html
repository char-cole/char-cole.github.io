art review: Months in the Gregorian Calendar

Month Tier List (ranked by value of rent paid)

Note 1: For the sake of simplicity, February is considered to be 28.25 days in the following calculations. In non-leap years (that is, most of them), this actually makes February look slightly less problematic than it actually is.

Note 2: These calculations are tied to the world as it is at the time of writing (2020), which means long-term corrections in the length of a year are not relevant. A year is considered to be simply the sum of its days, which is exactly 365.25 in this article.

The average (mean) non-February month is 30 days, 15 hours, 15 minutes, and 50 seconds long. I will refer to this as the NFM (Non-February Mean):

( ( 30 x 4 ) + ( 31 x 7 ) ) / 11 = 30.636

The average month including February is 30.4375 days long. This is nearly 5 hours shorter than the NFM, and is less useful in certain contexts.

  • S Tier: 31-day months

    These months offer the most time for rent paid. These months are more than 8 hours longer than the NFM - this is an entire night's sleep and then some. For this reason these months truly offer exceptional value. Luckily for us, slightly more than half of the months are 31 days.

    • January
    • March
    • May
    • July
    • August
    • October
    • December
  • B Tier: 30-day months

    Unfortunately these months are nearly two-thirds of a day shorter than the NFM and as such they are simply not a good value. By living through one of these months, we pay for two entire nights of sleep we don't get. There's no way a month this short could be considered A Tier - frankly, I'm not sure they're even good enough for B Tier. I do not enjoy these months and find they only look good in comparison to February. These months comprise one-third of the year, which, if we're going by the standard expectation of spending one-third of one's day working, is a roughly acceptible sacrifice.

    • April
    • June
    • September
    • November
  • F Tier: February

    February is a scam. While it might seem nice and neat that it's exactly four months long most years, that's simply not what a month represents. By being only 28.25 days long, February robs the renter of days of their time. We pay our landlords for sunrises that don't exist. We pay for movie nights where nothing is watched. We pay for an entire weekend that evaporates before we can even imagine it. I cannot ever recommend living through February.

    • February
