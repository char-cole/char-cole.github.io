tier list: Months in the Gregorian Calendar

The Length of a Year

There are two ways to measure a year: actual length and average length.

Actual length is simply the actual number of days in a year, which varies between common and leap years. Actual length is useful for comparing the value of a given month in a specific year with other months in that same calendar year. For exmaple, comparing the value of rent paid in October 2019 with November 2019.

Average length is useful for comparing value over an arbitrary number of years, no matter how many leap years are in it. This would be quicker for comparing the value of months over a period of 5 years, for example.

Calculating an Average

There are always several ways to calculate an "average". The most common method is the mathematical mean, which is just adding up each value and dividing by the number of items. This is useful for calculations, though it doesn't always translate as well to a relatable experience. It doesn't necessarily represent the most common value you could expect to find - this is the mathematical mode. The mode can show you, for example, exactly what the most likely actual month will be.

The mode month would be 31 days, because there are more months with 31 days in them than with any other value. But this isn't actually very useful when determining the value of a month. It undersells how good these months are by declaring them the baseline and overstates how bad other months are. By using a different measurement, we can appreciate how the good months give us more than the average month.

I will be ranking months according to mean calculations, which will result in a non-whole-number of days. But even after deciding that, there is still no one right way to calculate the mean. I will make calculations for two different means that are useful in different contexts:

  • Twelve Month Mean (TMM)

    We always divide a calendar year into twelve months, so this value is simply found by dividing the number of days in a calendar year by 12. Like the mean length of a year, this is most useful for comparing the value of an arbitrary number of months that may include any number of Februaries.

    Common Year 365 days 30.4167 days/month
    Leap Year 366 days 30.5 days/month
    Mean Year 365.25 days 30.4375 days/month

  • Non-February Mean (NFM)

    February is an outlier, and if we're not looking at an entire year, we can ignore it for certain comparisons. When we pay rent for a month, we're not actually paying for one-twelfth of the year, we're just paying until there are no more days left in the month. Therefore, when we're comparing a set of months that doesn't include a February, such as a 6-month lease starting in May, this is a useful value to use.

    Year Excluding February 337 days 30.6364 days/month

    The NFM is nearly 5 hours longer than the mean TMM. It's important to value these hours.

The Tier List

Since there are only 3 different kinds of months (31, 30, or February), several tiers are empty. For a reminder, the order of tiers from best to worst is: S A B C D F

S Tier: 31-day months

These months offer the most time for rent paid. These months are more than 8 hours longer than the NFM - this is an entire night's sleep and then some above the average non-February month.

These months shine even more against the TMM. S Tier months are exactly half a day longer than the mean month in a leap year. In a common year they clock in at just under 14 hours of above-average time.

  • January
  • March
  • May
  • July
  • August
  • October
  • December

C Tier: 30-day months

These months are simply not a good value. Compared to the TMM of a leap year, each of these months falls 12 full hours short. In a common year they're 10 hours under the average - still more than a good night's sleep lost. But against the NFM these months are each missing over 15 hours.

I don't recommend spending a month's rent on a C Tier month given the choice. They're not a disaster, but there's just no upside to losing half a day. Unfortunately these months comprise a full third of the year:

  • April
  • June
  • September
  • November

F Tier: February

February is a scam. No matter what metric you use, February robs the renter of days of their time. In the best-case scenario, a leap year, the 29-day February is 36 hours shorter than the average month - from Sunday morning to Monday night. But in common years, three out of every four, time lost to February comes out to more than 58 hours. This is long enough to take a one- or two-night trip to almost anywhere in the world near a major airport.

In February, we pay our landlords for sunrises that don't exist. We pay for movie nights where nothing is watched. We pay for an entire weekend that is forgotten before we can even imagine it. I cannot ever recommend paying rent in February.
